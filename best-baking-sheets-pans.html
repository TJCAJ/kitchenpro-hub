Types of Baking Sheets & When to Use Them

Half Sheet Pans (18" x 13")

  • Best for: Cookies, roasted vegetables, sheet cakes
  • Why we love them: Fits standard home ovens perfectly
  • Pro tip: The most versatile size for home bakers

Quarter Sheet Pans (13" x 9")

  • Best for: Small batches, toaster ovens, reheating
  • Why we love them: Perfect for smaller households
  • Pro tip: Great for side dishes and appetizers

Jelly Roll Pans (15" x 10")

  • Best for: Swiss rolls, bar cookies, shallow roasting
  • Why we love them: 1-inch rim prevents spills
  • Pro tip: Essential for liquid batters

Rimmed vs. Rimless

  • Rimmed: Better for liquids, easier to handle
  • Rimless: Easier to slide cookies off, better browning
  • Our recommendation: Have both styles

Care & Maintenance Tips

Cleaning Best Practices

  • Hand wash recommended: Even dishwasher-safe pans last longer with hand washing
  • Baking soda paste: Removes stubborn stains and buildup
  • Avoid abrasives: Steel wool damages non-stick coatings
  • Dry immediately: Prevents water spots and corrosion

Storage Solutions

  • Store vertically to save space
  • Use pan protectors to prevent scratching
  • Nest carefully to avoid damage
  • Keep in dry location to prevent rust

When to Replace

  • Warped pans cause uneven baking
  • Scratched non-stick surfaces lose effectiveness
  • Rust spots indicate structural compromise
  • Dark staining affects baking performance
